A good approach is to copy the gamification principles, not the exact UI.

Duolingo works because it uses a few strong loops:

fast feedback
daily habit building
small rewards
visible progress
gentle consequences
streak protection
social pressure, but low friction
For Radpreps, I’d recommend adding gamification in phases.

Best gamification features to start with
1. Streaks
Most effective habit builder.

daily study streak
streak freeze / streak repair
weekly streak summary
Why it works:

makes users return regularly
creates emotional attachment to progress
Good for Radpreps:

“Study 3 days in a row”
“Maintain your prep streak”
2. XP / Points
Reward every completed action.

Example:

answer question correctly: +10 XP
complete a quiz: +50 XP
finish a practice set: +100 XP
review missed questions: +20 XP
Why it works:

gives immediate reinforcement
makes progress feel tangible
Tip:

don’t reward only correctness; reward consistent effort too
3. Levels / Ranks
Turn XP into progress tiers.

Example:

Bronze
Silver
Gold
Diamond
Master
Or:

Level 1–50
Why it works:

gives users a sense of identity
long-term motivation
4. Progress bars
Very important and simple.

Use progress bars for:

course completion
daily study goal
mastery per category
quiz completion
Why it works:

users love seeing “how close am I?”
5. Achievements / Badges
Use them for milestone moments.

Examples:

First quiz completed
7-day streak
100 questions answered
90% accuracy in a topic
Completed a weak-area review
Why it works:

gives moments of celebration
encourages breadth and consistency
6. Daily goals
A strong retention driver.

Examples:

answer 10 questions
study for 15 minutes
review 5 missed questions
Why it works:

gives a clear achievable target
creates a repeat habit
7. Unlockable content
Make progress feel meaningful.

Examples:

unlock harder quizzes
unlock timed mode
unlock new categories
unlock review challenges
Why it works:

creates anticipation
makes learning feel like a journey
8. Leaderboards
Use carefully.

Best if:

based on weekly XP
based on friends/class cohorts
based on small groups
Avoid:

global leaderboards only
anything that discourages low performers
Why it works:

adds competition
but can hurt motivation if overused
9. Recovery mechanics
This is one of Duolingo’s smartest ideas.

Examples:

streak freeze
one free miss per week
“recover streak” after missed day
forgiveness tokens
Why it works:

reduces shame
keeps users from quitting after one miss
This is especially useful for education apps.

10. Encouraging feedback
Small text feedback matters a lot.

Examples:

“Nice work”
“You’re on fire”
“Almost there”
“Great recovery”
“Keep going”
Why it works:

makes the app feel alive
reduces friction
What I’d recommend for Radpreps specifically
Since Radpreps is an educational prep app, I’d prioritize:

Phase 1
XP
streaks
daily goals
progress bars
badges
Phase 2
levels
category mastery
streak freeze
weekly summaries
unlockable challenge modes
Phase 3
private leaderboards
study groups
seasonal events
limited-time challenges
Best design principles
For a prep app, gamification should:

encourage consistency
reward effort and improvement
not punish failure too harshly
feel motivating, not childish
fit the seriousness of exam prep
So avoid:

too many cartoon animations
aggressive popups
noisy reward spam
useless virtual currencies
A strong Radpreps gamification model
You could build around this loop:

user studies daily
earns XP
maintains streak
unlocks badges and levels
sees progress across weak topics
gets gentle reminders and recovery options
returns tomorrow
That is the Duolingo-style retention loop adapted for exam prep.

My recommendation: start with these 5
If you want the highest ROI:

streaks
XP
daily goals
progress bars
badges
That’s enough to make the app feel rewarding without overbuilding.
